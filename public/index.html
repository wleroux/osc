Baila Productions Pro (Montreal)

Refusant de se conformer au moule en vigueur, tentant toujours de pousser plus loin sa démarche artistique, la troupe Baila Productions se démarque par son originalité, tant au niveau de la danse qu'au niveau musical.

Rania Greiche (Montreal)

D’origine égyptienne, mordue de la danse depuis son enfance dans plusieurs styles; ballet, Hip-Hop et Baladi, la danse latine fut celle qui l’inspire le plus, et ce depuis bientôt 8 ans. Professeure, chorégraphe et danseuse professionnelle depuis 4 ans, elle a fait plusieurs compétitions et spectacles au niveau national et international d’autant plus que d’avoir participé à divers congrès & festivals dont le festival de jazz de Montréal & Festival juste pour rire, le congres de Los Angeles en 2012 et encore plus! Elle continue à transmettre sa passion avec son partenaire Rickey Zepeda depuis maintenant 3 ans. Organisatrice, Directrice & fondatrice d’une grande compétition à Montréal, Salsa Fever Dance Competition & passionné de ce qu’elle fait, voici Rania Greiche

Rickey Zepeda (Montreal)

Il danse depuis maintenant 8 ans et est également professeur et Chorégraphe depuis 5 ans. D'origine Guatémaltèque, il a dansé sur scène National & International entres autres au Chili, Los Angeles 2009 & à Chicago. Il a participé à plusieurs congrès et Festivals de danse afin de partager sa passion de la danse Latine et continue jusqu'aujourd’hui de le faire avec sa partenaire, Rania Greiche en n'ayant qu'un objectif, vous transmettre le plaisir et la joie de danser. Tout récemment, février 2015, il obtient la première place dans la catégorie duo Men’s styling avec son partenaire Adriano, nous revenant avec le titre de champion mondiale de la grande compétition World Salsa Summit à Miami.

Salsa-Force (Ottawa)

UOSalsa, the official salsa club at the University of Ottawa, is a student run club that, as a social organization, strives to provide students and community members with a fun and welcoming environment to learn the fundamentals and more of salsa and other Latin dances, and as a competitive dance team, trains intensively in order to develop a higher level of technique and skill among all its members. The founder and current coach of UOSalsa is Jose Sandoval of Salsa-Force (www.salsa-force.com). Jose has had over 10 years of experience where he has been able to perfect techniques, tips, and tricks that he is willing to share with all his students in order to take them to the next level. Jose has instructed and performed at international events throughout North America and Asia including in Ottawa, Toronto, Montreal, Miami, Singapore, Hong Kong, Taiwan, and Vietnam, and he has led the UOSalsa performance team in their participation in many events including several performances within the Ottawa/Gatineau region and at the Canada Salsa and Bachata congress in Toronto. UOSalsa believes that “Salsa is not just a dance, it’s a lifestyle.”

Golden Flame Dance Company (Ottawa)

Aletta and Abby are two different young and dedicated individuals from two different cultures but were raised with the great talent of dance. Aletta was born and raised in Russia and from age of 6 started studying dance from the best Latin, Ballroom, Flamenco, Bellydance, and Ballet school, and after many years of practice, she started teaching dance back home. At the same time in Nigeria, Abby started his dance career from the street as Hip- Hop and Latin dancer and then moved to Canada where he studied other different dance styles like Ballet, Contemporary, Tap, Jazz, Ballroom, Salsa, Bachata, and Zouk from famous schools and teachers across Ontario. Abby and Aletta met in Ottawa on the dance floor of Rahim's salsa Friday night and since then became a furious duo mixed with so many different dance styles. And today they dance, compete and perform, teach Salsa and Bachata partner work and footwork technique, choreograph together, give workshops across Canada.

The young but already well-known dance company Golden Flame Dance is the creation of Abby’s hard work and creativity and Aletta has become a part of it as well. The couple is happy to be performing for Ottawa on Ottawa’s Salsa Convention bringing all the talent and accumulated experience in one incendiary and fiery Salsa show!

Isabelle & Jacques

Dancing together since 2013, Isabelle and Jacques are passionate amateur dancers who have won several prizes including the Canadian Amateur Bachata Championship in 2014 and 2015, the 1st place in both salsa and bachata at the Montreal Salsa Convention in 2015, and the 3rd place in the salsa cabaret category at the World Salsa Summit in 2015 and 2016 in Miami. Trained by influential local and international coaches, they complement their respective dance background by delicately harmonizing the free-spirit and effervescence of modern jazz and oriental dancing with the lines and structure of ballroom dancing.

Dansant ensemble depuis 2013, Isabelle et Jacques sont des danseurs amateurs passionnés qui ont remporté plusieurs prix, dont le championnat amateur canadien de bachata en 2014 et 2015, la 1ère place en salsa et bachata à la convention de salsa de Montréal de 2015, et la 3ème place dans la catégorie salsa cabaret lors du sommet mondial de la salsa de 2015 et 2016 à Miami. Formés par des entraîneurs locaux et internationaux influents, ils complémentent leur expérience respective de danse en harmonisant délicatement l'esprit libre et l’effervescence du jazz moderne et de la danse orientale avec les lignes et la structure de la danse de salon.

Studio X (Ottawa)

Studio X Ottawa is an eclectic dance, fitness and space rental studio located downtown Ottawa. The owners, Nadège and Soul (co-directors of the former Blackanese Mambo Dance Team), is a dynamic duo of Haitian and Asian background as well as a couple on stage and off who have been dancing for about 17 years (10 years of which they have been dancing together), and they have competed and performed in various dance events in North America. Their passion for dance have made them try many different dance styles and take on new challenges to gain a variety of experiences and reinvent themselves to bring you an unique blackanese flavor on stage.

Studio X Ottawa est un studio éclectique de danse, de mise en forme et de location de salles situé au centre-ville d’Ottawa. Les propriétaires, Nadège and Soul (co-directeurs de l’ancienne équipe Blackanese Mambo Dance Team), est un duo dynamique d’origine haïtienne et asiatique qui sont également un couple autant sur la scène que dans la vie de tous les jours. Ils possèdent tous deux environ 17 ans d’expérience en danse (dont 10 ans à danser ensemble) et ont participé à des compétitions ainsi que donné des prestations dans divers événements en Amérique du Nord. Leur passion commune pour la danse sous toutes ses formes les a poussés à essayer différents styles de danse et à entreprendre des nouveaux défis en vue d’acquérir une variété d’expériences et de se réinventer pour vous offrir une saveur afro-asiatique unique sur scène.

Trena Graham (Halifax)

Based in Halifax, Trena has been teaching in various classes, dance schools and clubs since 2001, where she has applied her extensive ballroom and Latin training. She founded Trena’s Dance Studio in 2006; where she is artistic director, choreographer and pole fitness instructor. She has also been a local and national judge for the Atlantic and Canadian Pole Fitness Championships.

Trena focusses primarily on Salsa and Bachata, she has performed these locally and is excited to hit the national stage. Her technique and style are always impressive but it’s her energy, enthusiasm and engaging smile on the dance floor that are most memorable.

Recently, she has taught at the 2015 Toronto International Bachata Congress, 2015 Calgary Salsa Congress and most recently the 2016 Montreal Bachata Festival.

Harold Rancano and Regan Hirose (Winnipeg)

Harold Rancano and Regan Hirose, are a Latin dance couple best known for their energetic, and acrobatic flair on stage. Based out of Winnipeg, Manitoba, the flavorful couple has achieved plenty of success on a national and international level. Most notably they are the current World Champions for Bachata Cabaret having won gold at the World Latin Dance Cup in Miami, Florida; and are also the current Canadian Bachata Champions having won gold at the Canada Salsa Congress in Toronto, Ontario. The couple has also competed and won various competitions in Salsa and Bachata in San Francisco, Los Angeles, Calgary, and Saskatoon.

Aside from competition, the duo also directs Cubanisimo Dance Company in their hometown, where they train and coach their teams and students, 3 of which won gold at the 2015 World Latin Dance cup. They also coach and mentor various teams outside of the city by both teaching and creating choreography.

Also known for their connection and unique choreography, the couple has travelled to several festivals and congresses to share their vast knowledge of styles and various latin rhythms such as; Salsa on 1, Salsa on 2, Bachata, Pachanga, Cuban Salsa, Cha cha cha and Lifts & Tricks. Whether it’s privates, group classes or to coach teams, the couple has the experience to adapt to any teaching environment.

Corinne Tardieu (Montreal)

Corinne danse depuis son enfance. Son premier contact avec la danse fut à l’âge de trois ans où elle passait des cours entier dans les bras du professeur de ballet de ses sœurs. Depuis, la dance est devenue une partie importante de sa vie. Danse folklorique haïtienne, ballet, jazz, moderne, flamenco, salsa, elle a touché à toutes les danses. À l’adolescence, elle approfondie sa passion pour la danse lors des 5 ans passés dans un programme de danse-étude. En 2001, elle découvre la salsa et y est accros depuis lors. Elle participe à de nombreux congres de salsa, compétitions et évènements au niveau national et international. En 2009, elle remporte deux des plus grande compétitions de salsa au canada lors des congres de Montréal et Toronto et représente le canada cette année lors du ``World salsa Championship`` où elle arrive à ce placer dans le top 10 de la catégorie ``on 1``. Depuis lors, elle est une figure féminine reconnue dans le milieu de la danse latine à Montréal.

Ritmo Baile (Ottawa)

Ritmo Baile School of Dance is an organization based in Ottawa, dedicated to introducing individuals to the world of Latin Dance through workshops, performances and competitions. Our mission is to use dance to promote self-confidence, exercise as well as being a vessel for self-expression. Ritmo Baile engages its students in a wide variety of dance styles, allowing the creativity in each individual to shine through. Our goal is to continue to not only create a sense of community amongst dancers, but also to promote art in a diverse city such as Ottawa.

Luc and Julie-Anne (Gatineau)

SalsaOlé opened its doors in 2006. Since then, its dancers have performed at many events in Canada and the United States.

SalsaOlé’s directors, Luc and Julie-Anne, are two times World Pro-Am Bachata Champions and two times World Pro-Am Salsa Vice-Champions. They are considered two of the most influential dancers and instructors in the National Capital Region and are travelling around Canada and the United States to teach, compete and perform. They are coached by Multiple World Champions Billy Fajardo & Katie Marlow from Fort Lauderdale, Florida, USA and train regularly with the most influential instructors in the world.

 

SalsaOlé a ouvert ses portes en 2006. Depuis, ses danseurs se sont produits à de nombreux événements au Canada et aux États-Unis.

Les directeurs de SalsaOlé, Luc et Julie-Anne, sont deux fois champions du monde pro-am en bachata et deux fois vice-champions du monde pro-am en salsa. Ils comptent parmi les danseurs et professeurs les plus influents de la région de la capitale nationale et ils voyagent au Canada et aux États-Unis pour enseigner la danse, participer à des compétitions et se produire en spectacle. Ils sont entraînés par les multiples champions du monde Billy Fajardo et Katie Marlow de Fort Lauderdale en Floride, aux États-Unis et s’entraînent aussi régulièrement avec les instructeurs les plus influents au monde.

San Tropez (Montreal)

With over 20 years of experience, Sonia Kyriacou and Moris Alvarenga are talented and renowned dancers, who have graced stages all across Europe and North America. Not only do they bring versatility and a magical connection to every performance, but their passion for Latin dance is an inspiration.

Based in Montreal, they direct the San Tropez Dance Centers, one of the largest Latin dance schools in Canada. They also host thousands of dancers every year at the prestigious Montreal Salsa Convention now celebrating it’s 12th year!

It is with great pleasure that they present their troupe, Descarga, a team of talented dancers whose experience is as diverse as their talent.

Steps Dance Studios (Toronto)

Angelo and Kathy began dancing together since 2009. Their passion for latin dancing has led them to start competing and performing together. They are the 2011 Chim Pum Callao Congress and the Canada Salsa Congress Bachata Champions and were finalist at the Toronto Bachata Festival.

They enjoy travelling and sharing their passion for dance and have performed at international events such as the All Star Salsa Europian Cruise and the Israel Salsa Congress. Together they have developed a unique style of bachata, have taught hundreds of classes and workshops, and have choreographed numerous routines for both professionals and amateurs alike.

Angelo De Torres is a professional dancer and choreographer and has had a love and appreciation for music since a very young age. The diverse forms of expression inherent in Salsa, Bachata, Pachanga and Latin Hustle is what captivated him to learn and share his passion. He has extensive training with respected international instructors such as Billy Fajardo, Katie Marlow, Nelson Flores, Tito Ortoz, Tamara Livolsi, Francesco Scalvenzi and Milko Succi. Angelo has been teaching and dancing since 2008 for Steps dance studio under the direction of Jennifer Aucoin and is now a co-director of Steps Studio Inc.

Kathy Dondish has had the passion for dance at an early age, when she took her first ballet class at the age of 6 and trained in gymnastics at age 11. Her love for latin dancing only grew stronger as of 2007 and hasn't stopped since.

Today, she is a performer, teacher and competitor. Having trained with top Toronto dancers as well as internationally has increased her drive to share her love on stage and in the studio.
